[LEARNING MODULE]: Governing Dynamics

ACCUMULATION = IN - OUT + GENERATION - CONSUMPTION

Before dealing with thermodynamics or fluid friction, a process engineer must ensure mass is conserved. Adjusting 'Feed Flow Rate' (Q) dictates inputs. 'Target Conversion' (X) defines the generation/consumption of limiting reactants. Imbalances will literally overflow the tanks.

  • Q (m³/hr): Volumetric Flow Rate. Directly proportional to pump CapEx.
  • X (%): Conversion Factor. (N_A0 - N_A)/N_A0. Approaching 100% requires exponential reactor volume (V).
  • Split Ratios: Mismanaging the recycle loop will cause steady-state accumulation.

P_PUMP / ρ + v²/2 = ΔZ * g + f(L/D)(v²/2)

Pushing fluid through a pipe generates friction. To maintain flow, a pump must supply enough head to overcome elevation changes (ΔZ) and friction loss (Moody friction factor 'f').

  • Re (Reynolds No.): ρvD/μ. Determines if flow is Laminar (< 2000) or Turbulent (> 4000).
  • CapEx vs. OpEx Tradeoff: Larger pipe diameter = less friction (lower OpEx for pumps), but higher upfront cost (CapEx).
  • Pump Sizing: Pump brake horsepower = (Q * ΔP) / (η). Inefficiencies generate waste heat.

k = A * e^(-E_a / (R * T))

Chemical reactions depend exponentially on temperature. Heating a Continuous Stirred-Tank Reactor (CSTR) increases reaction rate (k) dramatically, but risks runaway thermal explosions and costs massive energy.

  • T (Kelvin): System Temperature. The apex control variable.
  • System Runaway: Exothermic reactions generate extra heat, driving T higher, looping the Arrhenius function until node failure.
  • E_a (kJ/mol): Activation Energy. Catalysts synthesize pathways to map this value lower, saving thermal CapEx.
  • CSTR vs. PFR: Plug Flow Reactors handle kinetics differently across length than stirred tanks across time.

PROFIT = $ * [F_A0 * X] - (CapEx + OpEx(v²))

The ultimate test. Everything is a trade-off. Forcing 100% conversion costs near-infinite energy. Running pipes perfectly fast requires giant pumps. The thermodynamic "sweet spot" is an inverted parabola.

  • PID Automation: Proportional-Integral-Derivative controllers automate Y1-Y3 tasks. Kp (Response), Ki (Drift), Kd (Damping).
  • CapEx ($): Upfront capital expenditure (buying bigger pipes/tanks).
  • Net Present Value (NPV) & ROI: Market price fluctuations for feedstock define actual economic viability.
  • The Inverted U-Curve: The point at which marginal revenue of product = marginal cost of utilities.
